# Product

## Register
product

## Platform
web

## Users
One operator (the founder of GridLink) running LinkedIn outreach for a small
EV-charging-reliability company. They open this tool several times a day to
answer one question fast — "what's happening, and what needs me?" — then act on
it: approve a drafted message, see who accepted an invite, spot a stalled lead.
They are fluent in software, low on patience for ceremony, and usually checking
between other work. The secondary reader is a future teammate who inherits the
tool cold and must understand campaign state without a walkthrough.

## Product Purpose
Give the operator live, legible visibility into every outreach campaign — where
each lead sits in the funnel, what already went out, and what is waiting on a
human decision — so they never have to ask the agent or open LinkedIn to know
the state of their pipeline. Success is: a glance answers "who's where and what
needs me," and the next action is one click away.

## Positioning
The control room for outreach: every lead's position is visible and every
pending decision is one action away, without leaving the page.

## Brand Personality
Precise, calm, and honest. It reports real state plainly and never dresses up
an empty queue or a stalled lead. Voice is a competent colleague, not a
marketer: "13 awaiting connection," not "Your pipeline is thriving." Three
words: exact, quiet, trustworthy.

## Anti-references
- The generic AI-dashboard look: pure-black panels with a purple-to-blue accent,
  colored left-border stripes on every card, glowing shadows, identical stat
  cards in a row.
- Marketing-analytics dashboards that lead with a giant vanity number and a
  gradient sparkline instead of the actual work list.
- Anything that hides the data behind decoration or makes the operator hunt for
  the one row that needs them.

## Design Principles
- **State over story.** Show the real funnel and the real rows, not a summarized
  narrative. The data is the interface.
- **Surface what needs a human.** A pending approval is the most important thing
  on the screen when it exists, and invisible when it doesn't.
- **One glance, one click.** The answer is visible without scrolling; the action
  is reachable without navigating away.
- **Honest empties.** Empty and stalled states say what's true and what to do,
  never fill space with fake positivity.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Target WCAG 2.1 AA: 4.5:1 text contrast in every theme (verified against each
theme's own background, light and dark), visible keyboard focus on every
control, full keyboard paths for filter/sort/approve, and `prefers-reduced-motion`
honored. Data tables use real table semantics; status is never encoded by color
alone (always a label beside the dot).
